The class of June 13 was designed for you to realize and discuss the following facts about stereotypes:

1- Even if you dislike or disapprove of stereotypes, you still have many stereotypes of people from another culture. This was proven to you at the beginning of the class, when you spent about 20 minutes expressing a few stereotypes you have about people from certain nationalities.

2- Having stereotypes is a normal thing. All human beings hold stereotypes because our brain cognitively categorizes people into groups and assigns characteristics to these groups. This happens automatically, regardless of whether you want it to happen or not. 

3- Stereotypes necessarily have an element of truth in them. They may not be entirely "true", but they are likely not entirely "false" either.

4- Regardless of how "true" or how "false" a stereotype might be, it can still be very offensive, demeaning, discriminatory, or mean-spirited towards the people you stereotype. The reason for this is that stereotypes almost never represent 100% of the members of the group you are stereotyping. Hence, those that do not feel represented by your stereotype can take offense from you representing them in that way.

5- Although some stereotypes are "positive" ( this means that they portray a good image of the people you are stereotyping), even these "good stereotypes" are rarely 100% true. 

6- The key point when thinking about/discussing stereotypes is not whether you have them or not. If you are a human being, then you hold stereotypes about other people. The real key point is to assume a critical posture about the stereotypes you have. You should always question the source from where you took that stereotype and ask yourself questions such as :

" Is it really fair for me to represent other people with this stereotype just because I ________________insert the source of your stereotype here______________?"

" Is it possible that this stereotype I have might offend or discriminate against some of the people I am representing with this stereotype?"
